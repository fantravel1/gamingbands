Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about virtual bands, in-game concerts, and everything music inside video games.

Fictional bands in video games are musical acts that exist entirely within a game's universe. They aren't real-world bands — they're created by game developers as part of the game's world-building. Examples include SAMURAI from Cyberpunk 2077, the Squid Sisters from Splatoon, and K.K. Slider from Animal Crossing. These bands have their own lore, discographies, and sometimes even in-game concerts.

The Travis Scott Astronomical event in Fortnite (April 2020) holds the record for highest concurrent attendance at 12.3 million players. However, the Lil Nas X Concert Experience in Roblox (November 2021) accumulated over 33 million total visits across multiple performances, making it the most-visited in-game concert event overall.

The Virtual Band Index is GamingBands.com's crown jewel — a comprehensive, searchable database of every known fictional band, NPC music group, and player-created ensemble that exists inside video games. Each entry includes the band's game origin, genre, members, lore summary, discography, cultural impact score, and performance archive.

Games renowned for their fictional music cultures include Cyberpunk 2077 (SAMURAI, Us Cracks), Splatoon series (Squid Sisters, Off the Hook, Deep Cut), Animal Crossing (K.K. Slider), GTA series (radio station bands), Brutal Legend (entire world built on metal music), The Witcher 3 (bards and tavern music), Final Fantasy XIV (The Primals, in-game bard performances), and rhythm games like Guitar Hero and Rock Band which feature fictional venues and storylines.
